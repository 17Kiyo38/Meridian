A Quick Reintroduction

Update articles are our own version of a developer diary, where we showcase all of our recent work on Expanse or whatever other game or content we're currently working on at the time. Update articles were used quite some time ago to show off some work, but due to a lack of upkeep on the idea and poor formatting, they had been dropped for quite some time and are now being brought back to life! So, we hope you enjoy this new revival of our update articles, now including more content from DoubleDog_DareMe and Sors.

DoubleDog and the DareGlass

DoubleDog_DareMe has created his own brand to create his UI under within Expanse, known as DareGlass. DareGlass will be used as a centralized service to access all the necessary content for players across the universe. DareGlass is used both for the player's own HUD/UI, but also for the station services, which is fundamentally the available player interaction in stations of all sorts. Station services will, primarily, grant access, to dockyards, data logs, dealerships, and shipbuilders. All of the content you'll see on this article is subject to change, however.

Dealership UI by Fingeroo and DoubleDog_DareMe
Dockyard UI by Fingeroo and DoubleDog_DareMe

Along with the station services UI, there is also the HUD and DareGlass for player information, which is built off the DareGlass home screen.

" This is the Home Screen for the Dareglass, which is essentially the in-game equivalent of the smart phone. You see all of your very essential & some not-so-essential GUIs from here. The Home Screen is meant to show you the most important info; stuff like your mission objectives, your health, etc. So you won't have to navigate a whole GUI to get to the info you need- you just open the DareGlass and see it. "

Sors and a Storyline

Sors, aka Symmakhia, has been with us for quite some time and is our main aid in creating the backstory of Expanse, its content (such as ships, stations, and even certain rocks) and more. Recently, Sors has been a big part of our development as he's the link that helps us create a viable world universe for Expanse. His story building helps the build team with the construction of systems, stations, the small details in asteroids, the specifics of ships, and much more. His work also includes helping the art team with the design process of ads or station content through the historical details of manufacturers and companies with the universe of Expanse. Not only does Sors work on the story building, but also the editing of these update articles, which happen to be riddled with infuriating mistakes which prompt him to effectively add a sentence at the end of a paragraph explaining just how martyrish proofreading this is. whoops
